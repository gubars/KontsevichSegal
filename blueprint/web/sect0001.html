1 The domain of complex metrics (KS Section 2)

This chapter is the blueprint for Section 2 of Kontsevich–Segal, Wick rotation and the positivity of energy in quantum field theory (arXiv:2105.10161), formalized in KontsevichSegal/ComplexMetrics/. The paper introduces, for a \(d\)-dimensional real vector space \(V\), an open domain \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\) of allowable complex-valued quadratic forms (“complex metrics”), generalizing positive-definite inner products. The Shilov boundary of this domain contains the real Lorentzian metrics and no other nondegenerate real metrics.

Section 1 of the paper, the Introduction, is motivation and has no formalizable content of its own. It recalls ordinary quantum mechanics (states as rays in a Hilbert space, the evolution \(U_t = \mathrm e^{\mathrm i H t}\), and positivity of energy as positive-semidefiniteness of the Hamiltonian, equivalently \(U_t\) being the boundary value of a bounded holomorphic function on the upper half-plane), the path-integral picture (a field space, an action functional, and its oscillatory measure), and the two views of ‘Wick rotation’. The rest of the paper makes these precise by other means, through the domain of complex metrics, so the Introduction states no definitions, axioms, or theorems of its own. The observables \(\mathcal O_x\) and vacuum expectation values sketched there are an informal preview, footnoted in the paper as an oversimplification for the introduction, and are formalized in Section 5. The Shilov boundary is recalled as a standard notion of several complex variables, cited from Hörmander and used heuristically; it is cited infrastructure rather than Kontsevich–Segal content, and the specific results about the Shilov boundary of \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\), that its nondegenerate real metrics are exactly the Lorentzian ones, together with the underlying Siegel-domain and Lagrangian-Grassmannian setup, belong to Section 2. Section 1 therefore contributes no formalization node; it is motivation together with previews of content formalized in Sections 2 and 5.

1.1 Allowable complex metrics

Definition 1 Allowable complex metric, Hodge-star form; KS Definition 2.1

Lean declarations

  • KontsevichSegal.Hodge.IsAllowableHodge

On a \(d\)-dimensional real vector space \(V\), a quadratic form \(g \colon V \to \mathbb C\) is an allowable complex metric if, for all degrees \(p \geq 0\), the real part of the quadratic form

\[ \Lambda ^p(V^*) \longrightarrow |\Lambda ^d(V^*)|_{\mathbb C}, \qquad \alpha \mapsto \alpha \wedge *_g\, \alpha \]

is positive-definite. Here \(*_g\) is the Hodge star operator of the complex metric \(g\), and \(|\Lambda ^d(V^*)|_{\mathbb C}\) is the complexified twisted determinant line, with orientation-reversing automorphisms of \(V\) acting antilinearly; the real part is positive when it lies on the ray of positive real volume elements of the twisted line.

The formalization places the form on real \(p\)-vectors, mirrored from the \(p\)-covectors by the complementary-blade correspondence, and normalizes by the principal square root of the Gram determinant of \(g\) in a real coframe, which the twisted line determines up to a positive real factor. On the blades of a diagonalizing basis the values are positive real multiples of the paper’s values \((\lambda _1 \cdots \lambda _d)^{1/2} \prod _{i \in S} \lambda _i^{-1}\), so positive-definiteness of the real part is the same condition on both sides.

Definition 2 Allowable complex metric, working definition: the angle condition
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • AngleCondition
  • AllowableComplexMetric
  • QC

A tuple of complex numbers \((\lambda _1, \dots , \lambda _d)\) satisfies the angle condition if every \(\lambda _i\) is nonzero, no \(\lambda _i\) lies on the non-positive real axis, and

\[ |\arg (\lambda _1)| + |\arg (\lambda _2)| + \dots + |\arg (\lambda _d)| \; {\lt}\; \pi . \tag {4} \]

An allowable complex metric on a finite-dimensional real vector space \(V\) is a symmetric nondegenerate \(\mathbb R\)-bilinear form \(g \colon V \times V \to \mathbb C\) for which there exist a basis \((y_i)\) of coordinates on \(V\) and coefficients \((\lambda _i)\) satisfying the angle condition with

\[ g \; =\; \lambda _1 y_1^2 + \lambda _2 y_2^2 + \dots + \lambda _d y_d^2 . \]

The collection of allowable complex metrics on \(V\) is denoted \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\); in the paper it is an open subset of the complex vector space \(S^2(V^*_{\mathbb C})\). This diagonal characterization serves as the definition of allowability throughout the formalization, since Definition 1 requires the Hodge star operator.

Lean declarations

  • defn_2_1_equiv_angle_condition

A quadratic form \(g \colon V \to \mathbb C\) satisfies Definition 1 if and only if it can be written, in some basis of \(V\), as \(\sum _i \lambda _i y_i^2\) with nonzero coefficients \(\lambda _i\), not on the negative real axis, satisfying the angle condition (4) of Definition 2.

Here \(g\) ranges over the symmetric \(\mathbb C\)-valued forms on \(V\) whose Gram determinant in a real coframe is not on the closed negative real axis, the standing requirement of the paper’s Section 2 under which the Hodge star and the principal branch of \((\det g)^{1/2}\) are defined; the diagonalizability of \(g\) is part of the conclusion, not an assumption.

Proof ▶

(Paper, p. 10.) A form satisfying Definition 1 with \(p = 1\) has \(\operatorname {Re}\bigl((\det g)^{-1/2} g\bigr)\) positive-definite, which allows simultaneous diagonalization of the real and imaginary parts of \(g\) over \(\mathbb R\): the positive-definite combination is an inner product, either part is represented in it by a self-adjoint operator, and the spectral theorem provides an eigenbasis diagonalizing both parts at once. In a diagonalizing basis the form \(\alpha \mapsto \alpha \wedge *_g \alpha \) on \(\Lambda ^p(V^*)\) is diagonal with values \((\lambda _1 \cdots \lambda _d)^{1/2} \prod _{i \in S} \lambda _i^{-1}\) on the basis \(p\)-forms \(e_S^*\); positivity of all their real parts for every subset \(S\) is precisely condition (4). For the converse of this last step, the diagonal values lie in the open right half-plane, where principal arguments add exactly, with no correction by a multiple of \(2\pi \); comparing the subset \(S = \{ i : \arg \lambda _i \geq 0\} \) with its complement then bounds \(\sum _i |\arg \lambda _i|\) below \(\pi \), and no \(\lambda _i\) is negative real, its argument being a difference of two arguments in \((-\pi /2, \pi /2)\).

Lemma 4 Values form a convex cone; KS p. 9
✓

Lean declarations

  • not_neg_real_axis

For \(g \in Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\) one has \(\max _i \arg \lambda _i - \min _i \arg \lambda _i {\lt} \pi \), so as \(v\) runs through \(V\) the values \(g(v)\) form a closed convex cone in \(\mathbb C\) disjoint from the open negative real axis. In particular \(g(v)\) is never real and negative.

Proof ▶

From (4), if \(\arg \lambda _j {\gt} 0 {\gt} \arg \lambda _k\) then \(\arg \lambda _j - \arg \lambda _k \leq \sum _i |\arg \lambda _i| {\lt} \pi \); hence all values \(\lambda _i y_i^2\) lie in a closed convex cone of opening angle less than \(\pi \), which contains no two opposite rays and avoids the open negative real axis. Nonnegative combinations stay in the cone.

Lemma 5 The volume element; KS p. 7

Lean declarations

  • volume_element_positive

For \(g \in Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\) the determinant \(\det g\) (of the Gram matrix in any basis; it is invariantly defined up to multiplication by a positive real number) is not real and negative, and the square root \((\det g)^{1/2}\) can be chosen with strictly positive real part. This fixes the holomorphic volume element \(\mathrm{vol}_g = (\det g)^{1/2} |dy^1 \cdots dy^d|\).

Proof ▶

In a diagonalizing basis \(\det g = \lambda _1 \cdots \lambda _d\), whose argument is \(\sum _i \arg \lambda _i \in (-\pi , \pi )\) by (4); a base change multiplies the Gram determinant by \((\det P)^2 {\gt} 0\). Since \(\arg \det g \in (-\pi ,\pi )\), the principal square root has \(|\arg | {\lt} \pi /2\), i.e. positive real part.

If a complex metric on a \(d\)-dimensional real vector space \(V\) satisfies condition (4), then the configuration space \(\mathrm{Conf}_k(V)\) of \(k\)-tuples of distinct points of \(V\) is contained in the holomorphic envelope of the Wightman permuted extended tube \(\mathcal U_k(V) \subset (V_{\mathbb C})^k\).

Proof ▶

(Paper, appendix to Section 2.) Identify \(V\) with \(\exp (\mathrm i \Theta /2)(\mathbb E) \subset \mathbb M_{\mathbb C}\) where \(\Theta \) lies in the generalized octahedron \(\Pi _0\) cut out by (4). One shows \(\exp (\mathrm i \Theta /2)(\mathbf x) \in \mathcal U_k\) when \(\mathrm{Re}(\Theta )\) lies on a coordinate axis (\(\Pi _{00}\)), then extends to the convex hull \(\Pi _0\) of \(\Pi _{00}\) by Bochner’s tube theorem; the reduction of the \(d = 2\) case to the standard tube theorem is Lemma 13.

1.2 Structure of the domain \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\)

Definition 7 The fiber \(\Pi (V)\): trace-norm ball; KS p. 11
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • TraceNormLtOne

For a positive-definite inner product \(g_0\) on \(V\), let \(\Pi (V, g_0)\) denote the convex open set of \(g_0\)-self-adjoint operators \(\Theta \colon V \to V\) with trace-norm \(\| \Theta \| _1 = \sum _i |\theta _i| {\lt} 1\), where \(\theta _i\) are the eigenvalues; that is, the interior of the convex hull of the rank-one orthogonal projections. Since \(V\) carries no inner-product structure in the formalization, self-adjointness with respect to some positive-definite \(g_0\) is encoded equivalently as diagonalizability over \(\mathbb R\): an eigenbasis with real eigenvalues \(\theta \) satisfying \(\sum _i |\theta _i| {\lt} 1\).

Proposition 8 Parametrization and contractibility; KS Proposition 2.4
#

Uses

  • Definition 7
  • Definition 54

Lean declarations

  • QC_parametrization
  • QC_contractible

\(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\) is a fibre bundle over the space of positive-definite inner products on \(V\) whose fibre at \(g_0\) is \(\Pi (V, g_0)\). Equivalently, choosing a reference inner product,

\[ Q_{\mathbb C}(V) \; \cong \; \mathrm{GL}(V) \times _{\mathrm{O}(V)} \Pi (V). \]

In particular, \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\) is contractible.

Stating this in Lean requires \(\mathrm{GL}(V)/\mathrm{O}(V)\) actions with associated-bundle machinery, an inner-product-space structure compatible with the algebraic setup of the formalization, and a topology on \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\), which is needed even to state contractibility; none are available in Mathlib.

Proof ▶

(Paper, pp. 10–11.) The diagonalization of Definition 2 presents \(g\) as a sequence of angles \(\theta _1 \geq \dots \geq \theta _m\) in \((-\pi ,\pi )\) and a decomposition \(V = V_1 \oplus \dots \oplus V_m\) with \(\sum _k \dim V_k \cdot |\theta _k| {\lt} \pi \), on \(V_k\) the form being \(e^{\mathrm i \theta _k}\) times a positive-definite real form. The subspace \(P = \bigoplus e^{-\mathrm i \theta _k/2} V_k \subset V_{\mathbb C}\) is canonical, carries a real positive-definite form \(g_0\), and \(V = \exp (\mathrm i \pi \Theta /2)(P)\) where \(\Theta \) is \(g_0\)-self-adjoint with \(\| \Theta \| _1 {\lt} 1\); condition (4) is exactly the trace-norm bound.

Proposition 11 Real allowable subspaces of a complex quadratic space; KS Proposition 2.6

Let \(Z\) be a \(d\)-dimensional complex vector space with a nondegenerate quadratic form \(g\), and let \(\mathcal R(Z)\) be the space of \(d\)-dimensional real subspaces \(A \subset Z\) such that \(g|_A\) belongs to \(Q_{\mathbb C}(A)\) (an open subset of the real Grassmannian). Then \(\mathcal R(Z)\) is contractible, and

\[ \mathcal R(Z) \; \cong \; \mathrm{O}_{\mathbb C}(Z) \times _{\mathrm{O}(Z_{\mathbb R})} \Pi (Z_{\mathbb R}). \]

Formalization requires the Grassmannian of real subspaces, \(\mathrm{O}_{\mathbb C}(Z)\)-actions, and the same associated-bundle machinery as Proposition 8. The result is needed for the Wick rotation of field operators in Section 5 of the paper and is deferred to WickRotation/FieldOperators.

Proof ▶

(Paper, p. 13.) The space \(\mathcal R(V; Z)\) of \(\mathbb R\)-linear embeddings \(f \colon V \to Z\) with \(f^*(g)\) allowable is simultaneously a principal \(\mathrm{GL}(V)\)-bundle over \(\mathcal R(Z)\) and a principal \(\mathrm{O}_{\mathbb C}(Z)\)-bundle over \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\). The contractibility of \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\) (Proposition 8) and the homotopy equivalence of both structure groups with the compact \(\mathrm O_d\) yield the contractibility of \(\mathcal R(Z)\).

The domain \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\) is holomorphically convex, i.e. a domain of holomorphy in \(S^2(V^*_{\mathbb C})\).

1.3 The Shilov boundary: Lorentzian metrics

Definition 14 Lorentzian signature
✓

  • IsLorentzian

A real symmetric bilinear form \(\varphi \) on \(V\) is Lorentzian (of signature \((d-1, 1)\)) if in some basis it diagonalizes as \(\sum _i s_i x_i y_i\) with each \(s_i \in \{ +1, -1\} \) and exactly one \(s_i = -1\).

Proposition 15 Lorentzian metrics lie on the boundary; KS p. 9
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 2

  • Definition 2

Lean declarations

  • lorentzian_on_boundary

A real Lorentzian metric \(\varphi \) on \(V\) lies on the boundary of \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\): it is a limit of allowable complex metrics (entry-wise on a fixed basis, i.e. in the canonical topology on the finite-dimensional space of forms), but is not itself allowable.

Proof ▶

Diagonalize \(\varphi = \sum _i s_i y_i^2\) with exactly one \(s_i = -1\) and perturb the negative coefficient to \(e^{\mathrm i (\pi - \delta )}\): the angle sum is \(\pi - \delta {\lt} \pi \), so the perturbation is allowable and converges to \(\varphi \) as \(\delta \downarrow 0\). That \(\varphi \) itself is not allowable follows from Lemma 4: \(\varphi \) takes a real negative value on the timelike basis vector.

Proposition 16 Only Lorentzian metrics lie on the boundary; KS p. 9
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 2
  • Definition 14
  • Definition 2
  • Definition 14

Lean declarations

  • only_lorentzian_on_boundary

If a real nondegenerate bilinear form \(\varphi \) on \(V\) is a limit of allowable complex metrics but is not positive-definite (the positive-definite forms being the real points of the open domain \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\) itself), then \(\varphi \) is Lorentzian. Equivalently, among real nondegenerate metrics, those of signature \((d-1,1)\), and no others, lie on the boundary of \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\).

Proof ▶

Diagonalize \(\varphi \) over \(\mathbb R\) in an orthogonal basis \(e_i\), rescaling so that \(\varphi (e_i, e_i) = s_i \in \{ +1, -1\} \); nondegeneracy makes every diagonal entry nonzero. Since \(\varphi \) is not positive-definite, at least one \(s_i = -1\). Suppose two signs were negative, say \(s_{i_0} = s_j = -1\) with \(i_0 \neq j\). On the plane \(W = \langle e_{i_0}, e_j \rangle \) one has \(\varphi (v, v) = -(x^2 + y^2)\), so \(\varphi \) is negative-definite on \(W\). Choose an allowable \(g\) whose entries on a fixed basis are within \(\varepsilon \) of those of \(\varphi \), with \(\varepsilon \) small enough that \(\operatorname {Re} g(v, v) {\lt} 0\) for every nonzero \(v \in W\); the entrywise closeness transfers to a uniform bound \(|\operatorname {Re} g(v, v) - \varphi (v, v)| \le C \varepsilon (x^2 + y^2)\) on \(W\). Writing \(g\) in its own diagonalizing basis, \(\operatorname {Re} g(v, v) = \sum _k (\operatorname {Re} \lambda _k) \beta _k(v)^2\). If two eigenvalues had \(\operatorname {Re} \lambda _k {\lt} 0\), then \(|\arg \lambda _k| \ge \pi /2\) for each, giving \(\sum _k |\arg \lambda _k| \ge \pi \) and contradicting the angle condition; hence at most one \(\operatorname {Re} \lambda _k\) is negative. The single linear condition \(\beta _{k_0}(v) = 0\) then cuts out a nonzero \(v \in W\) on which every surviving term has \(\operatorname {Re} \lambda _k \ge 0\), so \(\operatorname {Re} g(v, v) \ge 0\), contradicting negative-definiteness on \(W\). Therefore exactly one sign is negative and \(\varphi \) is Lorentzian.

Theorem 17 Two copies of the Lorentzian metrics on the Shilov boundary; KS p. 9

Lean declarations

  • two_copies_on_boundary

The Shilov boundary of \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\), regarded as a bounded domain in an affine variety as in the proof of Proposition 12, contains two disjoint copies of the space of Lorentzian metrics on \(V\), for an eigenvalue \(\lambda \) can approach the negative real axis either from above or from below. The two copies are interchanged by the complex-conjugation map on \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\). The faithful statement is not yet expressible in Lean. It requires: (1) induced forms on exterior powers and the Hodge star, for the affine-variety embedding \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V) \to \prod _{0 \leq p \leq d/2} \mathcal U(\Lambda ^p V)\) from the proof of Proposition 12, whose \(p = 0\) factor \((\det g)^{1/2}\) carries the branch that separates the two copies (in the naive closure inside \(S^2(V^*_{\mathbb C})\) the copies coincide); (2) Siegel domains and the Cayley transform, giving the bounded realization; (3) the Shilov boundary notion itself, the smallest compact subset of the closure on which every holomorphic function attains its supremum.

1.4 The two-dimensional case

Proposition 18 \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\) is a polydisc when \(\dim V = 2\); KS p. 15
#

Uses

  • Definition 2

Lean declarations

  • QC_two_dim_polydisc

When \(\dim V = 2\), the conformal class \((\det g)^{-1/2} g\) decouples from the volume element, and a nondegenerate complex metric is determined up to scale by its two null directions in \(\mathbb P(V_{\mathbb C})\); allowability puts one in each open hemisphere separated by the equator \(\mathbb P(V)\). The domain \(Q_{\mathbb C}(V)\) is thus a \(3\)-dimensional polydisc: one disc for each of the two complex structures (null directions) and one for the volume element.

Stating this in Lean requires the projective geometry of \(\mathbb P(V_{\mathbb C})\) and the conformal/volume decomposition of quadratic forms.
